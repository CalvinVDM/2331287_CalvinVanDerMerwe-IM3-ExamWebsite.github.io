Week 7 - The Ethics of UX / Review

This week we went through the Ethics of UX practice which I never thought about until now. When designing a user’s experience, I never really thought that ethics were involved as I have never touched on the ethics involving UX before. I always try to make the website cater to the user’s needs and feelings so that the user enjoys the website to the fullest and doesn’t find any problems in terms of navigating and trying to find certain things. When reading through the slides I thought about the other websites out there and what people are designing to produce or evoke emotions from the viewers.

I was intrigued when I saw a website that was made to be scary and so it was meant to make the user feel uneasy and uncomfortable. They did this by making the user feel as if they are being watched and by using a lot of darker colours and pop-ups that are unexpected. The other website was supposed to be based on anxiety and so it had many random pops ups filling the screen but those weren’t scary but rather annoying I can see how the creator tried to play with the concept, but I think that it’s difficult to pull off.

When looking at the slides I gained a deeper understanding of what it means to be ethical when creating UX and now I think about It a lot more in my designs and my thought process. The section on ethical concerns was good at highlighting concerns and things that I never thought off and it talks about problems that arise due to the usability and experiences our websites produce.
